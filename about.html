About me

Welcome to my website!

My name is Valérie Losier, and I am currently a Masters of Applied Science candidate in Mechanical Engineering at the University of Victoria (Victoria, Canada), working on sustainable energy solutions. I obtained my Bachelor of Science in Physics at McGill University (Montréal, Canada) in 2015 and promptly (excitingly) joined the Institute of Integrated Energy Systems at the University of Victoria to pursue my longtime aspiration to contribute to the advancement of sustainable technologies.

My current project involves computational modelling the catalyst layer of polymer electrolyte fuel cells. Take a look at my masters research page to learn more about fuel cells and my research.

My curiosity and diverse interests have led me to explore several research fields while studying at McGill University, and this culminated in various undergraduate research experiences and awards. The broad spectrum of research experience includes a project in experimental physics working on a prototype satellite instrument at the University of New Brunswick, theoretical and numerically intensive research in atmospheric science at McGill University as well as experimental work on a microwave breast cancer detection technique in the electrical engineering department at McGill University. Throughout my research experience I have discovered a passion for experimental and engineering projects and I am hoping to continue my education in mechanical engineering. Head to my projects page to learn more about my research contributions.

In the fall of 2013, I helped kickstart an NGO, Kids Code Jeunesse, which aims to bring computer science into mainstream education. As a programming teacher and education consultant, I produced the Scratch curriculum and supporting videos and material for educators. When I moved to Victoria, BC, I partnered with a few others to start the Kids Code Jeunesse movement on the west coast.

In my free time, I can be spotted playing piano, dancing, running, reading at one of the many beaches in Victoria and even developing my website.
