Kids Code Jeunesse

Kids Code Jeunesse is a Montréal based charity that aims to bring computer science in canadian schools by integrating coding projects with curriculum objectives. The younger students between the ages of 6-9 are exposed to computer science with Scratch, student of age 10-12 learn to design a web page with HTML/CSS, and students of age 12+ build simple projects in Python.

In 2013 as the organisation had just formed, I developed and tested the Scratch curriculum as well as supporting material for educators such as lesson movies, tutorials, and classroom projects. I also lead sessions for teachers and education students to share how to integrate programming into their classrooms.

When I moved to Victoria, I partnered with a few others to bring the coding in school movement to British Columbia. With my busy schedule I have mostly lead sessions in the Victoria Public Libraries. If you're an educator interested our program, head to the Kids Code Jeunesse website and show your interest by requesting a volunteer for your school.
